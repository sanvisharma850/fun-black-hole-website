The Cosmic Shield

Jupiter's immense gravity has deflected or captured thousands of comets and asteroids that would otherwise have struck the inner solar system. It is, in the most literal sense, a protector. Some large bodies in the universe just make everything safer around them.

02

The Great Red Spot

A storm system twice the diameter of Earth that has raged continuously for at least 350 years. It is shrinking slowly. Astronomers debate why it persists. Some forces are simply too large to stop all at once. Some storms last long enough to become something else entirely.

03

Frame-Dragging

Jupiter rotates faster than any other planet — once every 10 hours. This rapid spin distorts spacetime around it, a miniature version of the frame-dragging caused by black holes. Everything nearby is subtly swept into its rotation. It reshapes the space it occupies.
