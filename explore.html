Four phenomena that define the most extreme object in the universe.

01

Gravitational Lensing

Horizons bend light. Mass curves spacetime. Light follows that curvature. Around a black hole, photons can orbit in the photon sphere — a ring where gravity and velocity perfectly balance. Some light curves back on itself entirely, creating images of stars that don't exist in that direction.

02

Hawking Radiation

At the event horizon, virtual particle pairs form. One is captured; one escapes. The black hole loses mass imperceptibly. Over timescales longer than the current age of the universe, it evaporates entirely. Information is not destroyed — it is encoded in the radiation.

03

Time Dilation

Relativistic effects near a black hole: an observer falling in experiences nothing unusual at the moment of crossing. From outside, they appear frozen forever, red-shifted into invisibility. Two clocks at different gravitational depths tick at different speeds. This is not metaphor.

04

Spaghettification

Stretching lengthwise and compressing laterally — drawn into a thin strand of matter falling toward the singularity. Tidal forces near a stellar black hole do this to any extended object. The physics is real. The name, unexpectedly, is also real.
